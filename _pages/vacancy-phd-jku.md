# PhD Student Position

## Position Title
**Open PhD Position (30h, 4 years)**

## Organizational Unit
DarTwin Project - Johannes Kepler University (JKU) Linz, Austria

## Job Duties

We are seeking a highly motivated PhD student to join the DarTwin project at Johannes Kepler University Linz. The successful candidate will:

- Conduct research in condensed matter physics, focusing on quantum materials and experimental techniques
 - Conduct research on Digital Twins, model-driven engineering, computational modelling, and simulation methods
 - Develop and apply modelling workflows, simulation pipelines, and data-driven analysis techniques
 - Publish and present scientific results in journals and at conferences
 - Write a dissertation
 - Collaborate with international partners at the University of Antwerp
 - Contribute to the development of software tools and modelling artifacts for Digital Twins

## Qualifications

- **Required**: Diploma/Master's degree in Computer Science, Software Engineering, Systems Engineering, Computational Modelling, Information Technology, or a closely related field
- Strong interest in Digital Twins, modelling, simulation, and software-based system engineering
- Experience with programming (Python, MATLAB, Julia, or C/C++), data analysis, and modelling/simulation tools is preferred
- Very good command of English (spoken and written) (C1 level)
- Knowledge of German is an advantage
- Ability to work independently and as part of an international team
- Strong academic record

## What We Offer

- A dynamic and international research environment
- Access to computing resources and collaboration with modelling and software engineering groups
- Opportunities for professional development and networking
- Competitive salary according to the collective agreement (minimum €3,578.80 gross/month, 14 times per year)
- Full-time position (30 hours/week) for 4 years
- Collaboration with leading research groups in digital modelling and software engineering
- Support for conference attendance and research activities

## Application Process

If you are interested in this position, please send an email with the subject line **"Application PhD - DarTwin"** to [stefan.klikovits@jku.at](mailto:stefan.klikovits@jku.at).

Please include:
- A brief statement of interest
- Your CV, including information about the grades you had as an undergraduate
- Transcripts from your Bachelor's and Master's studies
- Contact information for 1-2 references

**Note**: No need for a separate cover letter or certificates at the initial stage.

## Additional Information

This position is part of the DarTwin project, a collaborative research initiative between Johannes Kepler University Linz and the University of Antwerp. The project focuses on advancing our understanding of quantum materials through innovative experimental approaches.

The position is available immediately and will remain open until filled. The PhD program typically takes 4 years to complete.

For more information about our research, please visit our [research page]({{ site.url }}{{ site.baseurl }}/research/) or contact us directly.

We actively foster a diverse, inclusive, and respectful workplace. We warmly welcome applications from women, ethnic minorities, persons with disabilities, and people of all sexual orientations and gender identities.
